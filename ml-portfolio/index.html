Artifact 4

Understanding Explainable AI (XAI): Building Trust in Modern AI Systems

This artifact visually explains explainable AI, why transparency matters, the major challenges in interpretability, and how validation techniques and performance metrics help build trustworthy AI systems.

Objective To clearly present the importance of explainable AI and show how validation, monitoring, and performance metrics improve trust, accountability, and responsible AI use.
Tools Infographic design, AI research synthesis, explainability concepts, visual communication, structured content design
Introduction

This artifact focuses on explainable AI (XAI) and the growing need to make advanced AI systems more transparent, interpretable, and accountable for real-world use.

Description

The infographic defines explainable AI, highlights its importance in building trust, and organizes the topic into four major areas: challenges in explainability, validation and performance metrics, current solutions and techniques, and the role of monitoring in responsible AI deployment.

Process

I researched the major barriers to explainability in models such as GPT, Claude, Gemini, and LLaMA, then classified the content into clear visual sections. I structured the infographic to show the relationship between explainability challenges, evaluation methods, and current industry approaches for improving AI transparency.

Value Proposition

This artifact demonstrates my ability to analyze a complex AI governance topic, synthesize technical research into meaningful categories, and communicate it through a polished and accessible visual format.

Unique Value

It combines technical understanding, classification, research synthesis, and design thinking to present explainability as both a technical and ethical requirement for trustworthy AI systems.

Relevance

This work is highly relevant to modern AI and machine learning because explainability, validation, fairness testing, and monitoring are essential for deploying AI responsibly in domains such as healthcare, finance, education, and legal decision-making.
